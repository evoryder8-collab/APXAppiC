# Orbit Science Ledger

Plan version: `orbit-campaign-1.0.0`
Next scheduled review: 2027-01-15

Orbit is evidence-informed, not scientifically infallible. This ledger records the principle, APEX implementation, intended population, evidence confidence and known limitation. It avoids proprietary plan copying and does not claim endorsement by any coach or clinician.

## Predominantly controlled low-intensity running

- APEX application: Most running remains conversational. Quality work is limited and phase-specific.
- Intended for: Adult recreational runners in every campaign family.
- Evidence: [Rosenblat et al., 2025 network meta-analysis of training-intensity distribution](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39888556/) and [2022 systematic review](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34749417/).
- Confidence: Moderate.
- Limitation: Current evidence does not establish one universally best distribution for every athlete and intervention duration.

## Progressive exposure and load interpretation

- APEX application: Conservative duration growth, regular cutbacks, no aggressive simultaneous increase in volume and intensity, and no catch-up stacking.
- Intended for: All pathways, with slower defaults for inconsistent or returning runners.
- Evidence: Training-load research supports monitoring exposure, but [load metric limitations are substantial](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32991699/) and [acute-to-chronic workload ratio claims have conceptual pitfalls](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32502973/).
- Confidence: Moderate for progressive exposure, low for any single injury-prediction metric.
- Limitation: [Evidence connecting specific load changes to running injury remains limited](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30534459/). Orbit does not predict injury.

## Strength coordination

- APEX application: Preserve useful APEX strength, separate quality running from demanding lower-body work where possible, and reduce conflicts instead of deleting strength by default.
- Intended for: Hybrid Athlete Marathon and users already following Transition or Main Phase.
- Evidence: [2024 systematic review and meta-analysis of strength methods and running economy](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38165636/).
- Confidence: Moderate to high for running-economy support in studied populations.
- Limitation: Methods, runners and intervention durations vary. No individual improvement is guaranteed.

## Tapering

- APEX application: Reduce volume before the event while preserving familiar rhythm; remove catch-up work.
- Intended for: Campaigns reaching taper and race week.
- Evidence: [Wang et al., 2023 systematic review and meta-analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37163550/).
- Confidence: Moderate.
- Limitation: Optimal taper magnitude depends on prior workload, athlete and event.

## Fueling rehearsal

- APEX application: Practise familiar pre-run, during-run and recovery foods during long runs. Show exact deltas and require explicit application. Never introduce an untested product in race week.
- Intended for: Long-duration campaign sessions and race preparation.
- Evidence: [2022 food-first carbohydrate review](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35231883/) and the [IOC supplement consensus](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29540367/).
- Confidence: Moderate.
- Limitation: Needs and gastrointestinal tolerance are individual. APEX does not interpret medication or prescribe a supplement protocol through Orbit.

## Fitness-readiness boundary

- APEX application: A short factual questionnaire can defer strenuous campaign assignment and recommend professional review. It never provides medical clearance.
- Intended for: Adults entering Marathon Campaign.
- Evidence: [ACSM and FIMS preparticipation screening consensus](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25391096/).
- Confidence: High that a non-medical product must retain this boundary.
- Limitation: A questionnaire cannot diagnose, clear or determine medical fitness.
